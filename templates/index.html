Shariah Financial Metrics Calculator

📌 What is Market Value?

Market Value (MV) refers to the total worth of a company’s shares based on its stock price.

💡 Why is Market Value important?

It serves as a reference point for financial screening in Islamic finance. Debt, liquid assets, and revenue sources are measured against this value to ensure fairness.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: How do I calculate Market Value?
    A: Multiply the stock price by the number of shares outstanding.
  • Q: Why not use total assets instead?
    A: Market Value reflects real investor perception, making it more dynamic than static total assets.

💰 What is Total Revenue?

Total Revenue refers to the total income a company earns from all business activities.

💡 Why does Total Revenue matter?

Shariah screening evaluates whether revenue comes from permissible sources and ensures that a company isn’t profiting from haram activities such as gambling, alcohol, or interest-based banking.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: What if only part of a company’s revenue is from haram activities?
    A: If haram income exceeds 5% of total revenue, the company may be deemed non-compliant.
  • Q: Does revenue include investment profits?
    A: Yes, but interest-based investment gains must be excluded or purified.

💳 What is Accounts Receivable?

Accounts Receivable (AR) refers to money owed to a company by customers for purchases made on credit.

💡 Why does AR matter in Shariah compliance?

Islamic finance discourages long-term credit dependency because it can involve interest-bearing delays, which contradict Shariah principles.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: Is it haram for companies to have Accounts Receivable?
    A: No, but companies must ensure prompt settlement to avoid interest-related risks.
  • Q: How can companies maintain Shariah compliance with AR?
    A: By minimizing overdue credit payments and avoiding interest charges.

⚠️ What is Debt?

Debt refers to borrowed money that a company must repay, often with interest.

💡 Why is Debt a concern in Shariah compliance?

Islam strictly prohibits riba (interest-bearing loans). Companies must keep debt levels below a specific threshold to maintain compliance.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: What types of debt violate Shariah compliance?
    A: Loans with interest (riba) are non-compliant. Islamic finance prefers equity-based funding.
  • Q: How do companies avoid non-compliant debt?
    A: By using Islamic financing methods such as Murabaha, Sukuk, and Ijara.

💵 What is Cash?

Cash includes money held in bank accounts, liquid assets, and any readily accessible funds.

💡 Why does Cash matter in Shariah compliance?

Islamic finance assesses cash liquidity because excessive idle cash can indicate reliance on interest-bearing savings, which is non-compliant under Shariah law.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: Can holding large amounts of cash be a problem?
    A: Yes, if the cash is sitting in interest-based accounts, the earnings from it would not be compliant.
  • Q: How can companies keep their cash Shariah-compliant?
    A: By keeping funds in Islamic banking accounts that avoid riba (interest-based earnings).

📈 What is Interest-Bearing Capital?

Interest-Bearing Invested Capital refers to funds placed in financial instruments that generate interest (riba).

💡 Why does Interest-Bearing Capital matter in Islamic finance?

Shariah law strictly prohibits earning money from interest-based investments. Companies must ensure their financial holdings avoid non-compliant instruments.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: What financial assets are considered non-compliant?
    A: Bonds, conventional savings accounts, and fixed-income securities that pay interest.
  • Q: How can companies ensure their investments are halal?
    A: By using Islamic investment vehicles like Sukuk (Islamic bonds) and Shariah-compliant stocks.

⚠️ What is Haram Revenue?

Haram Revenue refers to money earned from activities Islam prohibits. Examples include:

  • Interest-based banking (Riba)
  • Alcohol, gambling, and pork-related businesses
  • Adult entertainment or unethical industries

💡 Why is this important?

Islamic finance prohibits profiting from haram sources. If haram revenue exceeds 5% of total revenue, companies may need to purify earnings or risk non-compliance.

🧐 Common Questions

  • Q: What if a company earns only a small percentage from haram sources?
    A: Scholars permit companies to purify earnings if they are under the 5% limit.
  • Q: How can companies handle non-compliant revenue?
    A: By donating impure earnings to charity and limiting reliance on haram business streams.
